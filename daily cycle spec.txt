The Daily Cycle — Both Pathways
The master branch question

At day rollover, before anything else, Fable asks one question: is price sitting at external structure, or in the middle of nowhere?

Fab_NearExternal() checks Sydney's high and low against the last external HTF extreme (prior-day/prior-week rollover pools, or the live structural swing — whichever is nearer). Exactly one side aligned → ONE-SIDED model. Neither side aligned (or both, ambiguously) → TWO-SIDED model. This single check, made once at Sydney's window close, decides which of the two pathways below the rest of the day will follow.
Pathway A — ONE-SIDED liquidation model

Applies when Sydney's tested extreme sits at real external structure — meaning that extreme is already the true one, and price can only go the opposite way.

Direction: decided by the raid itself. The 00:00–01:00 raid spends the aligned side — low raided at external structure → DAY_BULL; high raided at external structure → DAY_BEAR. A raid of the non-aligned side is contradictory evidence and is ignored, not inverted.

Trade #1 — the raid-shift entry (~01:00). The raid is itself a structural shift — order flow just changed. This is a live, named, tradeable window (dayTypeSetBar, evidence floor 0.9 for the first 3 bars). It is ridden through the Sydney/early-Asia session and force-closed at a fixed uncertainty hour (default 06:00 GMT, Fab_GenesisExit()) — win, loss, or scratch, unconditionally — because at that hour the rest of the day's script is genuinely unknown. Holding through the classification stretch is a different, uncalibrated bet the system has no evidence for.

07:00–12:00 — classification, not trading. No named window, no evidence credit. The Asia range is watched: does price continue expanding it, or give it back? This single measurement — net progress at noon, primarily confirmed by whether price retraces through the Asia range's own midline (asiaMid/midlineRetrace) — splits the rest of the day into exactly two scripts.

If CONSERVATIVE (price milled, gave the break back to the midline): Trade #2 is the noon expansion — the mill-then-expand pattern resolves at 12:00, evidence floor 1.0 if the midline actually confirmed the mill, 0.8 if only the fallback threshold did.

If AGGRESSIVE (price kept expanding to noon): the leg from the Asia break to the noon extreme gets its own midline (aggLegMid) — the same law, one session nested inside the first. Trade #2 here is different in kind: the 12:00–15:00 leg is a scripted counter-move, not neutral ground — the counter-direction is the expected trade (day evidence inverts: counter +1.0, aligned −0.3), confirmed against the reversal window. Trade #3 is the 15:00 resumption, evidence floor 1.0 if the counter-leg confirmed at its own midline, 0.6 if not.

Both variants converge from there: expansion runs hard through the evening, window evidence staying live through DC_LATE (~20:00–21:00 GMT), before the close-window hygiene rules take over.
Pathway B — TWO-SIDED liquidation model

Applies when the open sits diagonally between external structure points — nowhere near either extreme. Both sides of Asia will trade during the day; the first raid is deliberate inducement, not a decision.

Direction: not from a raid — from HTF structural bias (st.externalStruct, falling back to st.trend) — because the raid mechanism doesn't apply when neither side is at real structure. This is read immediately at Sydney's close, before any raid happens.

The inducement sweep. On a bullish two-sided day, Sydney's high — the opposite extreme — gets swept first (tempSet/tempPx), forming a temporary high. This is manipulation, not confirmation, and it is honestly labeled as such: not discarded, but held in reserve as fuel for later. (Bearish days are the exact mirror: Sydney's low sweeps first, forming a temporary low.)

Asia range builds on the opposite side (the low, for a bullish day) as price expands away from the temp extreme. The midline between the temp extreme and this Asia opposite extreme (twoSidedMid) is the expected retrace target around the London open.

London tries to print the real low/high by sweeping the Asia opposite extreme. Whether that sweep is the true low of day depends on the same law used everywhere in this system: does it actually reach external structure?

    If yes — London's sweep IS the true low, confirmed on the spot (trueSet, truePx).
    If no — deferred. The US session raids London's own range next, and that sweep gets the identical external-structure test. Whichever sweep — London's or the US session's — actually tags real structure is the one that prints the true low. If neither ever does, the day stays honestly unresolved rather than guessing.

The viable trade: once the true low prints, the objective becomes the temporary high from the overnight inducement sweep — the fuel that was held in reserve. Evidence floors at 0.7 the moment the true low confirms, rising to 1.0 once price actually reclaims the temp extreme (reclaimed) — that reclaim is the clean expansion path for the rest of the day.

(Bearish two-sided days: temp low first, Asia high builds, London or the US session tries to print the true high, objective becomes the temp low.)
